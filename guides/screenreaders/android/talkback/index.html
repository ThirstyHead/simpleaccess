🤖 Native Android Access

The voice of the Android ecosystem.

TalkBack is the screen reader included on Android devices. It gives you eyes-free control of your phone or tablet, allowing you to interact with apps and the web using spoken feedback. Like other mobile tools, TalkBack uses specialized gestures—taps, swipes, and multi-finger movements—to help you "feel" the digital interface. Let's master the "Big Six" TalkBack skills.

1. Launch and Close

TalkBack is built into your system's accessibility suite. You can toggle it quickly using hardware shortcuts or voice commands:

  • Volume Key Shortcut: Press and hold both volume keys for 3 seconds. (Note: You may need to enable this shortcut in Settings first.)
  • Google Assistant: Say "OK Google, turn on TalkBack" or "Turn off TalkBack."
  • Manual Method: Open Settings > Accessibility > TalkBack and toggle the switch.

2. Mute (Silence)

If TalkBack is reading a long block of text and you need it to pause immediately without deactivating the screen reader:

  • To Silence: Tap the screen once with one finger or press any volume key. This stops the current speech buffer instantly.

3. Navigate by Headers

TalkBack uses "Reading Controls" to change how swipes behave. This allows you to meet WCAG SC 1.3.1 by jumping between landmarks.

  • Change navigation type: Swipe up or down with three fingers until you hear "Headings." (Alternatively, swipe up then down with one finger).
  • Jump: Once "Headings" is selected, swipe down with one finger to move to the next heading, or up to go back.

4. Navigate by Links

Quickly find actionable items to satisfy WCAG SC 2.4.4.

  • Set to Links: Swipe up or down with three fingers until you hear "Links."
  • Jump: Swipe down with one finger to move through every hyperlink on the current screen.

5. Navigate Form Fields

Entering data on mobile requires navigating directly to inputs, checkboxes, and buttons.

  • Set to Controls: Swipe up or down with three fingers until you hear "Controls."
  • Jump: Swipe down with one finger to focus on the next form element.
  • Interact: Double-tap anywhere on the screen to activate the focused field and bring up the keyboard.

SimpleAccess Insight: Reading Controls

The Reading Controls menu is the Android equivalent of the iOS Rotor. It is a dynamic "dial" that lets you choose if your finger swipes move you character-by-character, word-by-word, or between major landmarks like headers and links. If a navigation type is missing, you can customize the list in TalkBack Settings > Customize menus > Reading controls.

Testing Tip: Set your reading control to "Links" and swipe down to find the Elements Index link at the bottom of this page!

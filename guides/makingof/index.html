The Making Of

Collaborative Creation

SimpleAccess.io isn't just a site about accessibility—it is a living experiment in Human-AI Collaboration. Explore how we combined human intent, standards-based requirements, and AI execution to build a web that delights all of the senses.

🏗️

The SMACSS Foundation

From the very first line of code, we chose a Scalable and Modular Architecture for CSS. By categorizing our rules into Base, Layout, and Modules, we ensured that the AI could understand the "DNA" of the site, allowing for consistent updates without breaking the global design.

📌

The Baseline Methodology

To experiment safely, we established "Known Good" Baselines. Each major version (from 0.1 to 0.5) acted as a checkpoint. This allowed the human partner to guide the evolution of the site, reverting to stable states whenever the AI's suggestions strayed from the vision.

📜

Standards as the Source of Truth

We didn't just guess at "best practices." We integrated the WHATWG HTML Living Standard and WCAG 2.1 directly into our prompts. The AI acted as a librarian of standards, ensuring every element—from the Anchor tag to the complex Canvas—was semantically correct.

🔄

Conversational Refinement

Accessibility isn't extra work; it's a conversation. We refined every lede, adjusted every header color (like matching the MS Word Blue to our Navy), and verified every ARIA landmark through constant feedback loops.

⏳

The Timeline of Progress

Building this site was a journey of five distinct chapters. Each phase represented a deepening of our partnership and the site's complexity:

  • The Genesis: Establishing the mission and the Homepage.
  • Atomic Semantics: Crafting the HTML element guides.
  • Productivity for All: Engineering the MS Office section.
  • Fixed Layout Focus: Mastering the PDF section.
  • The Four Pillars: Synthesizing the WCAG section.

"Accessibility is already here..."

Our collaborative process proves that AI is a powerful tool for inclusivity when guided by Human Values. By focusing on the user's experience of sight, sound, and touch, we've created a documentation series that is as accessible as the standards it describes.
